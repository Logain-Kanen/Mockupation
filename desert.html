Career Spotlight: Welders

Welders, Cutters, Solderers, and Brazers are all similar jobs, having to do with joining and separating metal components together using heat and various methods of welding

Welder Tasks

  • Welding Components: The most important part of the job, welding parts together; both flat and curved pieces, corners, and vertical areas
  • Safety: Welding itself is a more dangerous job, so proper understanding of both safety gear and procedures is the first priority
  • Non Heat Based: Beyond heat, there are some metal pieces that may need to be bent, grinded, or cut, handling power tools needed for that is also important.
  • Soldering: Melt and apply solder to fill holes, indentations, or seams.
  • Tools: There is a large number of tools associated with welding, and knowing how to use each and every tool is key to successful welding

Education & Skills Needed

  • High School Degree: For many of the entry jobs only a high school diploma is required
  • Post-Secondary: Very few entry jobs may have a Post-Secondary certificate requirement
  • Skills: Precise and steady movements are heavily required, as well as attention to detail.

Work Environment

  • Fieldwork: Most of welding work is done in the field, oftentimes outside
  • Office Work: Small amounts of office work are still present, mostly customer interaction

Salary & Career Outlook

  • Median Salary: Around $53,770 per year
  • High Pay: Over top 10% sits at around $82,040
  • Job Growth: Slower growth due to automation, but growth is still expected
