Career Spotlight: Construction

Construction is a varied and physically challenging career with multiple jobs linked to it, so a much more simplified version is: construction is the process of building or repairing structures.

Day to Day Construction

  • Basic Construction: Laying of brick, drywall, wood frames, and other building materials, as well as the unique steps for each material
  • Heavy Machinery: The use of heavy machinery is very common in construction, and the usage of these machines requires focus and awareness to avoid damage
  • Traffic Around Workzones: Construction will often interfere with the traffic of cars and people, signs and barriers need to be placed to manage this
  • Proper Estimations: Customer interaciton is important, as such communication with the client is critical

Education & Skills Needed

  • High-School Diploma: Construction is not a demanding job, only requiring a high-school diploma or equivalent
  • Advanced Degrees: Advanced degrees are rarely required, even then, only highly advanced construction fields will require them
  • Measuring: Precision of measurements is a critical part of construction
  • Physical: A primary part of construction if physical labor, so physical strength is important

Work Environment

  • Fieldwork: Almost all work is done in the field, on construction sites and mostly outside
  • Management: Construction managers will often also be present on the field, but they have a small amount of office work as management
  • Enviornment: The enviornment of construction is heavily varied, as construction can be done nearly anywhere. Like the suburbs, the city, even rural areas.

Salary & Career Outlook

  • Median Salary: Around $46,730 per year
  • High Pay: Even top 10% for basic construction jobs is $77,530
  • Job Growth: Incredibly fast growth is expected as demands for construction rises
